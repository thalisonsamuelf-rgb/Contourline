# Visceral Copy Quality Checklist

## Overview

Quality gate for visceral copy generated by the Copy Squad. Visceral copy uses sensory language, emotional triggers, and "felt sense" descriptions to create a physical and emotional response in the reader.

---

## Critical Checks (must pass ALL)

### Sensory Language Density
- [ ] At least 3 of the 5 senses are engaged (sight, sound, touch, taste, smell)?
- [ ] Descriptions create mental images (not abstract concepts)?
- [ ] Reader can "see" the scene described?
- [ ] Body-based language is used ("gut-wrenching", "spine-tingling", "chest tight")?
- [ ] Sentences are concrete and physical, not intellectual?

### Emotional Triggers
- [ ] Primary emotion is identified and deliberately targeted?
- [ ] Emotional intensity builds gradually (not from 0 to 10 instantly)?
- [ ] At least 2 distinct emotions are triggered across the piece?
- [ ] Emotional triggers are authentic (not manipulative or exploitative)?
- [ ] Reader empathizes with the situation described?

### "Felt Sense" Descriptions
- [ ] Pain is described as an experience, not a concept ("you toss and turn at 3am" not "you have insomnia")?
- [ ] Desire is described as a felt moment ("imagine opening your laptop to see..." not "imagine success")?
- [ ] Transformation is painted as a sensory shift ("the weight lifts from your shoulders")?
- [ ] Reader recognizes their own experience in the description?

### Pain Amplification
- [ ] Problem is felt viscerally before the solution is introduced?
- [ ] Pain description is specific to the target avatar's real life?
- [ ] Amplification stops before crossing into exploitation or cruelty?
- [ ] Cost of inaction is described in daily-life terms?
- [ ] Reader feels urgency to escape the pain state?

## Red Flags (auto-fail if present)

- Copy is entirely abstract ("unlock your potential", "achieve greatness")
- No sensory language in 3+ consecutive paragraphs
- Pain description is generic and not avatar-specific
- Emotional manipulation crosses into shaming or fear-mongering
- All tell, no show (states emotions instead of evoking them)
- Cliche overload ("like a weight off your shoulders" without fresh detail)
- Visceral language feels forced or purple-prose

## Visceral Copy Technical Checks

### Language Audit
- [ ] Ratio of concrete nouns to abstract nouns is at least 3:1?
- [ ] Active verbs dominate (not passive constructions)?
- [ ] Sentences vary in length (short punches mixed with flowing descriptions)?
- [ ] Metaphors and analogies are fresh (not recycled)?

### Ethical Guardrails
- [ ] Pain amplification is truthful (describes real situations)?
- [ ] No exploitation of trauma, disability, or protected characteristics?
- [ ] Reader is empowered by the copy (not made to feel helpless)?
- [ ] Solution positioning is honest about what it can deliver?

Read the copy aloud. Score each:

| Test | Pass/Fail |

4/5 passes required.
