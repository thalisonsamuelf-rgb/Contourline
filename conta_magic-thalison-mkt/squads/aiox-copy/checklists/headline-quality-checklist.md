# Headline Quality Checklist

## Overview

Quality gate for headlines generated by the Copy Squad. Covers all headline types: sales pages, ads, emails, articles, and landing pages.

---

## Critical Checks (must pass ALL)

### Specificity
- [ ] Contains a concrete number, timeframe, or measurable result?
- [ ] Names the target audience explicitly or implicitly?
- [ ] Promises a specific outcome (not a vague improvement)?
- [ ] Could NOT be used for a completely different product?

### Curiosity Gap
- [ ] Creates an information gap the reader wants to close?
- [ ] Hints at a mechanism, secret, or discovery without revealing it?
- [ ] Avoids giving away the full answer in the headline itself?
- [ ] Makes the reader ask "how?" or "what?"?

### Benefit Clarity
- [ ] Primary benefit is obvious within 3 seconds?
- [ ] Written from the reader's perspective (what they GET, not what it IS)?
- [ ] Benefit is desirable to the target avatar?
- [ ] Avoids feature-speak disguised as benefits?

### Urgency
- [ ] Contains a time element, newness indicator, or consequence?
- [ ] Urgency is organic (not forced or fake)?
- [ ] Reader feels a reason to read NOW vs. later?

### Uniqueness
- [ ] Would not be confused with a competitor's headline?
- [ ] Contains a unique mechanism, angle, or framing?
- [ ] Avoids cliches and overused formulas ("The Secret to...")?

---

## Red Flags (auto-fail if present)

- Vague promise with no specificity ("Get Better Results Fast")
- No numbers, timeframes, or concrete details anywhere
- No identifiable target audience
- Too clever or abstract (prioritizes wit over clarity)
- Over 20 words without strong justification
- Makes unbelievable claims without qualifier
- Relies entirely on curiosity with zero benefit

## Headline Type Variations

### Sales Page Headlines
- [ ] Works as a standalone promise?
- [ ] Supports a sub-headline for added detail?

### Ad Headlines
- [ ] Under platform character limits?
- [ ] Works without surrounding context?
- [ ] Pairs effectively with the visual?

### Email Subject Lines
- [ ] Under 60 characters (under 45 preferred)?
- [ ] Avoids spam trigger patterns?
- [ ] Generates curiosity in inbox context?

### Blog/Article Headlines
- [ ] Contains target keyword naturally?
- [ ] Would earn a click from search results?
- [ ] Delivers on its promise in the content?
