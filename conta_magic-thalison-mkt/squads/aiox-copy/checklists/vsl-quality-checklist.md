# VSL (Video Sales Letter) Quality Checklist

## Overview

Quality gate for Video Sales Letters generated by the Copy Squad. Use this checklist to validate a VSL script before recording or publishing.

---

## Critical Checks (must pass ALL)

### Hook (First 30 Seconds)
- [ ] Opens with a pattern interrupt or bold statement?
- [ ] Viewer knows WHO this is for within 10 seconds?
- [ ] Creates immediate curiosity or emotional resonance?
- [ ] Avoids generic intros ("Hey, my name is...")?

### Story Arc
- [ ] Has a clear beginning (problem), middle (journey), and end (transformation)?
- [ ] Personal or client story grounds the narrative?
- [ ] Emotional peaks are spaced every 2-3 minutes?
- [ ] Story connects directly to the offer (not tangential)?

### Offer Clarity
- [ ] What they get is explained in simple, concrete terms?
- [ ] Price is anchored against higher perceived value?
- [ ] Guarantee is stated clearly and confidently?
- [ ] Bonuses are introduced with their own mini-hooks?

### CTA Power
- [ ] Primary CTA is unmistakable and repeated at least 3 times?
- [ ] CTA uses action language tied to the desired outcome?
- [ ] Urgency or scarcity element supports the CTA?
- [ ] Final CTA includes a consequence of inaction?

## Red Flags (auto-fail if present)

- No clear CTA anywhere in the script
- More than 3 minutes pass without a re-hook or engagement device
- Story is generic and could apply to any product
- Claims are made without any supporting proof
- Script reads like a blog post (no visual/audio cues)
- Monotone pacing with no variation in energy
- Offer is buried or unclear after full viewing

## Agent-Specific Validation

- If generated by **Gary Halbert agent**: validate raw, direct language and personal storytelling depth
- If generated by **Frank Kern agent**: validate casual tone, "results in advance" framing, lifestyle proof
- If generated by **Russell Brunson agent**: validate epiphany bridge structure and stack presentation

---

## VSL-Specific Technical Checks

- [ ] Script includes visual direction notes (text overlays, B-roll cues)?
- [ ] Estimated runtime is appropriate for the offer price point (under $100 = 15-25 min, over $100 = 30-60 min)?
- [ ] Transitions between sections feel natural when read aloud?
- [ ] No jargon or insider language without explanation?
