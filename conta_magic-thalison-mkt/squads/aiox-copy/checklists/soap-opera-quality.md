# Soap Opera Sequence Quality Checklist

## Overview

Quality gate for Soap Opera Email Sequences generated by the Copy Squad. Validates episodic storytelling, emotional arcs, cliffhangers, and conversion mechanics across the full sequence.

---

## Critical Checks (must pass ALL)

### Cliffhanger Strength
- [ ] Each email (except the last) ends with an unresolved tension?
- [ ] Cliffhanger is genuinely intriguing (not forced or gimmicky)?
- [ ] Reader NEEDS to open the next email to get resolution?
- [ ] Cliffhangers escalate in intensity across the sequence?

### Emotional Arc Per Email
- [ ] Each email has its own internal emotional journey?
- [ ] Opens with an emotional hook (not a summary or recap)?
- [ ] At least one moment of surprise, empathy, or tension per email?
- [ ] Emotional tone shifts within the email (not flat)?

### Character Development
- [ ] Protagonist (sender or client) is introduced with human detail?
- [ ] Character has a flaw, struggle, or relatable vulnerability?
- [ ] Transformation is gradual and believable across the sequence?
- [ ] Reader bonds with the character more with each email?
- [ ] Supporting characters (mentor, antagonist) add depth?

### Callback Consistency
- [ ] Each email references the previous one naturally?
- [ ] Running threads or motifs connect the full sequence?
- [ ] Details introduced early pay off later (no loose ends)?
- [ ] Tone and voice remain consistent across all emails?
- [ ] Timeline of events is clear and non-contradictory?

## Red Flags (auto-fail if present)

- An email ends without any cliffhanger or forward momentum
- Character is flat with no development across emails
- Sequence reads like 5 separate emails, not one connected story
- Cliffhanger is resolved immediately in the next email's first line (no build)
- Emotional tone is identical in every email (no arc)
- Offer appears suddenly with no story connection
- Contradictory details between emails (broke continuity)

## Sequence-Level Checks

### Structure (typical 5-email Soap Opera)
- [ ] Email 1: Sets the scene, introduces character, creates first cliffhanger?
- [ ] Email 2: Backstory and emotional depth, deepens the problem?
- [ ] Email 3: Turning point or epiphany moment?
- [ ] Email 4: Hidden benefits or transformation revealed?
- [ ] Email 5: Urgency, final push, complete resolution + offer?

### Pacing
- [ ] Sequence does not front-load all the best content?
- [ ] Middle emails maintain momentum (no "sagging middle")?
- [ ] Final email has the highest emotional intensity?
- [ ] Send cadence is specified (daily recommended)?
