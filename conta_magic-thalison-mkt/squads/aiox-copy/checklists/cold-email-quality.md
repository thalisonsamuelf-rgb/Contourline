# Cold Email Quality Checklist

## Overview

Quality gate for cold email copy generated by the Copy Squad. Validates personalization, relevance, conciseness, and deliverability for outbound prospecting emails.

---

## Critical Checks (must pass ALL)

### Subject Personalization
- [ ] Subject line includes a personalized element (name, company, trigger)?
- [ ] Subject reads like a 1:1 message, not a broadcast?
- [ ] Under 45 characters for mobile display?
- [ ] Avoids spam trigger patterns (all caps, exclamation marks, "free")?
- [ ] Creates enough curiosity to earn the open?

### Opening Relevance
- [ ] First line references something specific to the prospect (trigger event, post, company news)?
- [ ] No generic openings ("I came across your company...", "Hope you're well...")?
- [ ] Demonstrates research — prospect knows this is not mass-sent?
- [ ] Opening earns the second sentence (not disposable)?
- [ ] Connection between the opener and the value prop is clear?

### Value Prop Conciseness
- [ ] Core value proposition is stated in 1-2 sentences?
- [ ] Benefit is specific to the prospect's role or industry?
- [ ] No jargon, buzzwords, or self-congratulatory language?
- [ ] Focus is on what the PROSPECT gains, not what the sender does?
- [ ] Social proof snippet (if included) is brief and relevant?

### CTA Specificity
- [ ] One clear, low-friction ask?
- [ ] Ask is proportional to the relationship level (not "buy my thing")?
- [ ] CTA is a question, not a demand ("Would a 15-min call make sense?" not "Book a demo")?
- [ ] Next step is easy to say yes to?
- [ ] No multiple asks competing for attention?

### Spam Score
- [ ] Email passes basic spam filter checks (no trigger words)?
- [ ] HTML is minimal or text-only?
- [ ] No images, tracking pixels, or heavy formatting?
- [ ] Links are minimal (0-1 max in first email)?
- [ ] Sending domain is warmed and authenticated (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)?

## Red Flags (auto-fail if present)

- Generic template with no personalization signals
- Over 150 words in the first email
- CTA asks for too much too soon (demo, purchase)
- Multiple links or attachments
- Self-focused copy ("We are a leading provider of...")
- No clear reason why the prospect was chosen
- Reads like marketing email, not personal message
- No follow-up sequence planned

## Cold Email Sequence Checks

### Sequence Structure (if multi-touch)
- [ ] Email 1: Value-first introduction with soft CTA?
- [ ] Email 2: Different angle, reference Email 1?
- [ ] Email 3: Social proof or case study approach?
- [ ] Email 4: Breakup or "am I off base?" email?
- [ ] 3-5 business days between touches?
- [ ] Each email can stand alone (not dependent on opens)?

### Personalization Tiers
| Tier | Elements | Use When |
|------|----------|----------|
| Tier 1 (Mass) | {{firstName}}, {{company}} | Large volume, lower intent |
| Tier 2 (Segmented) | + industry pain point, role-specific benefit | Mid-volume, moderate intent |
| Tier 3 (Hyper-personal) | + specific trigger, recent post, mutual connection | Low volume, high intent |

---

## Technical Checks

- [ ] Merge fields are properly formatted and tested?
- [ ] Unsubscribe/opt-out mechanism is included (CAN-SPAM compliance)?
- [ ] Physical address is present in footer?
- [ ] Sending volume is appropriate for domain reputation?
- [ ] Reply-to address is monitored?
