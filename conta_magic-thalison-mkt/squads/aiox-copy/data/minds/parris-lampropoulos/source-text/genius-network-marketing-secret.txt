Show Notes

There are basically only three ways to increase your sales: you can increase the number of customers you have, you can increase how much they buy from you, or you can increase how often they buy from you.

Little hinges swing big doors; by increasing each of those factors by 25% you can nearly double your sales.

You can create the incremental increase through a variety of means, including raising your prices, adding another channel, and optimizing.

An upsell is when you go to McDonald’s and you order a cheeseburger and they ask you, “Would you like fries with that?”.

Mailing your customers and contacting them more frequently is going to give you the incremental lift you need, but be careful when doing so.

Hire the best copywriter you can afford. Take your biggest problems and stresses and put them on to someone else.

There is nothing more important than customer retention, nothing.

You can get new people coming through your door everyday but if you can’t keep the people you have, it’s like bailing out a leaky boat.

If you bludgeon your list with pitch after pitch, you will begin to alienate them. Sometimes you have to provide information and ask for nothing in return.

80% free content, 20% sales pitch.

You want to make your sales emails and editorial content look and sound the same. Try to keep the energy similar as well.

Useful is boring. Your goal shouldn’t be to just be useful, your goal should be to WOW the hell out of them.

There are two components to WOWing someone: tell them something new and tell them something surprising. When you have novelty and surprise, you will get them addicted to your copy.
