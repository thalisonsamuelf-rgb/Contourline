## 1. FRAMEWORKS (Step-by-Step Systems)

### F-01: The 15-Step Sales Letter Formula
A complete step-by-step system for writing a direct mail sales letter from scratch.

1. Create a **FACT SHEET** about what you are selling
2. Create a **BENEFIT LIST** by translating facts into buyer benefits
3. Reduce your **OFFER** to writing on a **DEAL SHEET** (the sweetest proposition possible)
4. Write a **headline** that contains news + the promise of one or more benefits
5. If you have more than two hot benefits, put another in the **lead-in sub-headline**
6. Reveal exactly **who the letter is from** (name, occupation, address)
7. Begin the salutation: **"Dear Friend,"**
8. Write a **first sentence** that tells what's in it for the prospect if he reads
9. Use the next few paragraphs to **elaborate specifically** on your promises
10. Tell him **why** you are offering such a good deal
11. **Enumerate facts** that translate to benefits (list 1, 2, 3, etc.)
12. Write a true and believable **"reason why"** explanation
13. **Ask him to respond** and urge him to hurry
14. Sign off with **"Sincerely,"**
15. Write a **P.S.** that gives a little new wrinkle on one of the hot reasons

[SOURCE: zhkl_get_it_going.txt]

### F-02: The "By The Numbers" Selling System
Converting your best personal sales pitch into print.

1. **Record** all your phone conversations with prospects (with permission)
2. Keep recording until you forget you are being recorded
3. Get a recording of you at **"concert pitch"** (your best)
4. Have it **transcribed** by a typist
5. **Number each paragraph** (1 through N)
6. **Rate each paragraph** on a scale of 1-10 for influence on prospect
7. **Cut out** all paragraphs and divide into 3 groups: benefits, interesting facts, filler
8. **Throw away** group 3 (filler)
9. **Arrange** remaining paragraphs in rank from 10 down
10. **Discard** all paragraphs rated 5 or less
11. **Rewrite** remaining paragraphs -- clean up, don't change much
12. Write it as a **long memo** to a specific, visualized person
13. Insert highest-ranked facts as numbered list, then highest-ranked benefits as numbered list

[SOURCE: zjkj_by_the_numbers.txt]

### F-09: The Refund Reduction System
Multi-step approach to reduce refunds on high-priced info products.

1. Create a **great product** (put 2.5x normal effort: 100 hours instead of 40)
2. **Deliver fast** -- use next-day morning FedEx, not UPS Ground
3. **Bond with customers**: Call same day you get their order to thank them
4. Call the day after they receive product to confirm everything arrived
5. Use an **atlas** to find something interesting about their city for conversation
6. One week later, send an **unexpected free gift** (e.g., a putter for golf product)
7. Two weeks later, send another **expensive unexpected gift** (e.g., large hardbound book)
8. **Integrate a celebrity** into your product (harder to "stiff" a known person)
9. Send a **personalized letter from the celebrity** referencing the customer's local area
10. Notify customer of a **valuable gift** they will receive 2 weeks from today (1 week AFTER check is cashable)
11. Form a bond with a **"hungry banker"** for expedited check cashing
12. Consider **selling receivables to a "factor"** (get 70% of check value immediately)

## 2. INSIGHTS (Key Principles)

### I-01: What You Say > How You Say It
"What you have to say is enormously more important than how you say it." If you have nothing special to say, sweeten your offer BEFORE writing copy. If you found out someone won the Irish Sweepstakes, there is no way you could tell them that in a dull way.
[SOURCE: zhkl_get_it_going.txt]

### I-02: Salesmanship Multiplied
"Good advertising is simply salesmanship multiplied." You can't multiply zeros -- if you have no salesmanship ability, you'll end up creating stupid campaigns with dancing raisins and catchy jingles.
[SOURCE: zhkk_image_suicide.txt, zjkj_by_the_numbers.txt]

### I-03: The #1 Reason People Don't Buy
The #1 reason people don't buy is they don't want what you are selling. The #2 reason is they don't believe you. Strive for believability -- it is supremely important.
[SOURCE: zjkl_whats_good.txt]

### I-04: Image Suicide
Commit "image suicide" -- stop protecting your expert image and start being accessible, warm, human. The payoff: (1) psychological freedom to learn, (2) become more accessible to customers. "The girls who get the most dates (and the ads that make the most sales) are those that appear warm, non-threatening and accessible."
[SOURCE: zhkk_image_suicide.txt]

### I-05: Copywriting Is Secondary to the Offer
"Copywriting is secondary to the creation of a powerful, believable offer." A great idea man beats a great copywriter every time. "I'd rather have a great idea man on my team any day."
[SOURCE: zjkl_whats_good.txt]

### I-06: Just Say What You Have To Say
"One of the secrets of writing great copy is just saying what you have to say!" People pay ungodly amounts for this. Don't try to be clever -- just say it simply.
[SOURCE: zhkl_get_it_going.txt]

### I-07: The Most Valuable Skill
"The most valuable skill you can have in today's world, if you want to become independently wealthy, is the ability to write a great sales letter. No other skill even comes close."
[SOURCE: zjll_most_valuable_skill.txt]

### I-08: HITS = "How Idiots Track Success"
Website "Hits" are meaningless. What matters is conversion rate. A 40% conversion rate is ~10,000 times higher than normal -- achieved through COPY, not design.
[SOURCE: zjll_most_valuable_skill.txt]

### I-09: Plain Jane Websites Sell
"Make your website 'Plain Jane.' No graphics. No photos. No animation. Nothing that moves." Website specialists are like brochure designers -- all their work detracts from sales. Black type on yellow background is easiest to read.
[SOURCE: zjlk_personal_computer.txt, zjll_most_valuable_skill.txt]

### I-10: You Don't Have To Double Response To Double Profit
"You do not have to be twice as tall to see twice as far." Since the first big chunk goes to advertising and fulfillment expenses, you only need to sweeten your gross by ~15% to double your profit.

### I-11: Improve Substance, Not Image
"Improving your image is fine but, the main thing you need to do is improve your substance. Concentrate on making your product or service better." Value sends out ripples of positive karma that come back as money.

### I-12: Specificity Enhances Response
