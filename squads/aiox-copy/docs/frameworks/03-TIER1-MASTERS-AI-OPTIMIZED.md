### Bencivenga Fascination System

```yaml
fascination_formula:
  components: ["Specific benefit", "Curiosity gap", "Specificity"]
  
  weak_examples:
    - "How to make more money"
    - "Productivity secrets"
  
  strong_examples:
    - "The $47,000 mistake that 93% of investors make every month — and how to profit when they lose (page 34)"
    - "Why drinking 8 glasses of water per day may be DESTROYING your kidneys — and what to do instead"

fascination_types:
  contrarian:
    pattern: "Why [common belief] is completely wrong"
    use: "Challenge assumptions"
  
  specific_benefit:
    pattern: "How to [result] in [time] without [sacrifice]"
    use: "Clear value proposition"
  
  curiosity_gap:
    pattern: "The [interesting person]'s technique for [benefit]"
    use: "Borrow authority + create intrigue"
  
  fear_based:
    pattern: "The warning sign of [problem] that [%] ignore"
    use: "Activate self-preservation"
  
  proof_based:
    pattern: "[Institution] study reveals [discovery]"
    use: "Authority + news"
```

### Bencivenga Objection Breaking

```yaml
objection_method:
  step_1: "LIST all possible objections"
  step_2: "RANK by frequency and importance"
  step_3: "ANTICIPATE in copy (before reader thinks it)"
  step_4: "NEUTRALIZE with logic or proof"
  step_5: "TRANSFORM objection into benefit"

example:
  objection: "It's too expensive"
  anticipation: "I know you might be thinking that $997 is a lot..."
  neutralization: "But if this saves just 2 hours per week, that's 104 hours per year. At $100/hour, that's $10,400."
  transformation: "Actually, the 'expensive' choice would be NOT having this..."

use_cases:
  - "Bullets/fascinations for any project"
  - "Emails that need clicks"
  - "Copy facing many objections"
  - "Products requiring benefit explanation"
```
