## Core Principle

Show two futures in sequence:

1. the future where the reader does not act
2. the future where the reader acts now

Pain first, relief second.

## Structure

### Paragraph 1: Future without action

- anchor to a specific time horizon
- show the problem still present
- intensify the cost of delay
- end on regret, not mere frustration

### Paragraph 2: Future with action

- return to the same time horizon
- show the decision as turning point
- make progress concrete
- end on relief, gratitude, or regained control

## Best Use Cases

- final sales page close
- final email in a sequence
- closing section before CTA
- high-stakes offer decisions

## Guardrails

- Use only when the downside is real and defensible.
- Keep it specific, not melodramatic.
- Never fabricate impossible consequences.
