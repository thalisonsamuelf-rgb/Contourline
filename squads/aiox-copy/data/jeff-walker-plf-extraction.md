## CORE FRAMEWORK: Product Launch Formula (PLF)

### The Sideways Sales Letter Concept

**Original Definition** (from Launch, Chapter 4):
> "Instead of a single long sales letter, PLF 'turns the letter sideways' and spreads the persuasion across multiple pieces of content over time."

**Key Insight**: A traditional sales letter tries to do everything at once. The Sideways Sales Letter does the same thing, but gives time for mental triggers to work.

**Structure**:
1. **Piece 1**: The Opportunity - Show what's possible
2. **Piece 2**: The Transformation - Prove it works
3. **Piece 3**: The Ownership - Make them feel they already own it
4. **Piece 4**: The Offer - Present the opportunity to buy

## THE 9 MENTAL TRIGGERS (Chapter 5: "Tools of Mass Influence")

### 1. Authority
**Definition**: We follow people who demonstrate expertise and credibility.
**Application**: Establish authority in PLC1 through your origin story.
**How**: Share credentials, show results, cite experts, demonstrate deep knowledge.

### 2. Reciprocity
**Definition**: When someone gives us something of value, we feel the need to reciprocate.
**Application**: Each PLC must deliver so much value that the prospect thinks "if this is free, imagine the paid version."
**How**: Real tools, templates, actionable insights, answer questions without asking for anything.

### 3. Trust
**Definition**: We buy from people we trust.
**Application**: The PLC sequence builds trust gradually through value delivered repeatedly.
**How**: Be transparent about limitations, keep promises, show genuine vulnerability, maintain consistency.

### 4. Anticipation
**Definition**: The wait for something good can be as pleasurable as the thing itself.
**Application**: Each PLC ends creating anticipation for the next. Open Cart is the apex of anticipation.
**Example from book**: "Think of Apple launching a new iPhone - weeks of rumors and anticipation."
**How**: Create teasers, use countdowns, reveal information gradually.

### 5. Likability (Likeability)
**Definition**: We buy from people we like.
**Application**: Use personal storytelling in each PLC to create emotional connection.
**How**: Be genuine and authentic, show personality, share personal stories, find common ground.

### 6. Events and Ritual
**Definition**: Events create natural urgency and a sense of "happening now."
**Application**: The launch isn't just an offer - it's an EVENT that the community participates in together.
**How**: Transform the launch into an event, create specific dates that matter, use live streams and webinars.

### 7. Community
**Definition**: We are influenced by the behavior of the group we belong to.
**Application**: Comments in PLCs create community. Buyers become part of something bigger.
**How**: Create sense of belonging, show others like them succeeding, use private groups and discussions.

### 8. Social Proof
**Definition**: We follow what others are doing, especially people similar to us.
**Application**: PLC2 is where you stack social proof. During Open Cart, show buyers in real time.
**How**: Show testimonials from "normal" people, share numbers, highlight positive comments, use detailed case studies.

### 9. Scarcity
**Definition**: We value more what is rare or limited.
**Application**: Open/Close Cart is real scarcity. The cart closes and there's no way to buy after.
**Warning from book**: "Fake scarcity destroys trust. Always be 100% honest about limitations."

**3 Types of Scarcity** (from Launch):
1. Price goes up
2. Remove bonuses
3. Offer goes away completely

## PRE-PRELAUNCH (Chapter 6: "The Shot Across the Bow")

**Purpose**: Build awareness and anticipation before the prelaunch.

**The Classic Pre-Pre "Ask" Campaign**:
Ask your list their top questions about your topic. Example questions from book:
- "What's your #1 question about [TOPIC]?"
- "What's holding you back from [DESIRED OUTCOME]?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would you change about [PROBLEM]?"

**Key Function**: Pre-pre is a "shot across the bow" - it sets the stage for your offer and starts to build awareness and even anticipation.

## PRELAUNCH CONTENT SEQUENCE (Chapter 7)

### PLC #1: The Opportunity (The Journey) - WHY
**Timing**: Day 1
**Duration**: 15-30 minutes of content
**Focus**: Show the opportunity/transformation possible

**Elements**:
- Present the avatar's problem/desire
- Reveal that a solution exists
- Establish authority (your story)
- Deliver real value (first teaching)
- Create anticipation for PLC2
- CTA: comment, share, reply

**Triggers Activated**: Authority, Social Proof, Anticipation

### PLC #2: The Transformation - WHAT
**Timing**: Day 3-5
**Duration**: 20-40 minutes of content
**Focus**: Show the transformation in action

**Elements**:
- Recap the opportunity from PLC1
- Case studies and social proof
- Teach something implementable
- Answer main objections
- Create more anticipation
- CTA: engagement + sharing

**Triggers Activated**: Social Proof, Reciprocity, Trust

### PLC #3: The Ownership Experience - HOW
**Timing**: Day 5-7
**Duration**: 25-45 minutes of content
**Focus**: Make the prospect see themselves using the solution

**Elements**:
- Recap of previous learnings
- Deliver more educational value
- Paint the vision of the transformed future
- Answer final questions and objections
- Prepare for Open Cart
- Create urgency for the opening

**Triggers Activated**: Anticipation, Community, Events

## OPEN CART / LAUNCH SEQUENCE (Chapter 8)

### Day 1 - Launch Day
**Emails**: 2-3
**Focus**: Announce opening, create excitement

**Content**:
- Email 1: "WE ARE OPEN!" (morning)
- Email 2: "Everything works!" (initial response)
- Email 3: Q&A / addressing concerns

**Note from book**: "25% of sales happen on the first day"

### Days 2-5 - Mid-Launch
**Emails**: 1 per day
**Focus**: Maintain momentum, answer objections

**Content**:
- Additional case studies
- FAQ answering objections
- Behind the scenes
- Testimonials from new buyers
- Reminder of deadline

### Final Day - Cart Close
**Emails**: 3-5
**Focus**: Final urgency, last objections

**Content**:
- Morning email: Last day
- Afternoon email: Final hours
- Night email: Closing in X hours
- Final email: Last chance

**Note from book**: "50% of sales happen on the last day"

## LAUNCH TYPES

### Seed Launch (Chapter 9)

**Best for**: Validate product before creating, start from scratch
**Audience size**: Can be done with small list (100-500 people)

**From the book**: "The Seed Launch is for those who are just starting out or want to validate a new idea. You sell the product BEFORE creating it completely. Deliver live while collecting feedback and building the product."

**Process**:
1. Identify a problem/desire of your audience
2. Create an offer to solve it
3. Do a simple launch (can be emails only)
4. Sell to a small group
5. Deliver live, collecting feedback
6. Use feedback to improve

**Advantages**:
- Validates demand before creating
- Finances product creation
- Direct feedback from buyers
- Low risk

**Revenue Expectation**: $2K - $50K typical

**Jeff's First Seed Launch Story**: After partnership breakup in 2005, started over from scratch. Spoke at conference, sold 6 spots in "Product Launch Workshop". Invited entrepreneurial friends to join for free to reach critical mass of ~30 people. Surveyed students before each call about their burning questions. Ended up doing 9-10 calls instead of promised 5. This became the first Product Launch Formula course.

### Internal Launch (Chapter 9-10)

**Best for**: Launch to your own email list
**Process**: Classic PLF with full PLC sequence

**Advantages**:
- Full control of the process
- Higher margins (no affiliate commissions)
- Direct relationship with buyers

**Revenue Expectation**: $10K - $500K+ depending on list

### JV Launch - Joint Venture (Chapter 10)

**Best for**: Scale with affiliate partners
**Definition from book**: "In a JV Launch you have partners mail their lists and tell their people about your launch. If you make a sale to one of the people your partner sends your way, you pay your partner a commission."

**Process**:
1. Recruit JV partners
2. Provide promotion materials
3. Coordinate promotion dates
4. Execute PLF for combined audience
5. Pay commissions after sales

**Key Metrics**: EPC (Earnings Per Click) - Commission per click referred

**Commission**: Typically 40-50%

**Critical Rule from book**: "Don't test with your partners' lists. Do an Internal Launch first to prove your offer converts."

**Jeff's Results**: October 21, 2005 - First JV Launch of PLF did $600,000+ in sales. Built list from 0 to 15,000 in days through JV partners.

**March 27, 2008** - PLF 2.0 launch: "We hit a million dollars in sales in 53 minutes." Final: $3.73 million in 34 hours.

### Evergreen Launch

**Best for**: Automate continuous sales

**Process**:
- Record PLCs (or use text)
- Set up email automation
- Each new lead enters the sequence
- Personalized deadline for each lead
- Cart closes automatically

**Note from book**: "Jeff recommends doing live launches first before evergreen"

## BUSINESS LAUNCH FORMULA (Chapter 11 Original / Chapter 14 Updated)

### The 6 Keys

**Key #1: Always Deliver High-Value Prelaunch Content**
Not all prospects will buy. Creating hundreds or thousands of raving fans has far-reaching benefits in social media world.

**Key #2: Always Be Building Your List and Relationship**
Your list universe is the most valuable asset in your business.

**Key #3: Make More Than One Offer**
Sweet spot: 2-4 launches per year. One or two big JV Launches, others Internal Launches.

**Key #4: The Circle of Awesome**
Seed → Internal → JV → Repeat

**Key #5: Relaunches and Evergreen Launches**
Relaunches can be very effective. Evergreen for continuous sales.

**Key #6: Take Care of Your Clients and Launch to Them Again**
15x easier to sell to someone a second time than to get a new client.

## NEW FRAMEWORKS (Updated Edition 2021)

### Live Launches (Chapter 11 Updated)

**Definition**: Using live broadcasts to deliver some or all of your launch content.

**Advantages**:
- Viewers stay longer on live vs recorded
- Can react to questions in real-time
- Higher engagement
- Authenticity and transparency

**Compressed Timing** (Jeff's recent format):
```
Day 1: PLC #1 (Opportunity) - Live Broadcast
Day 2: PLC #2 (Transformation) - Live Broadcast
Day 3: PLC #3 (Ownership) - Live Broadcast
Day 4: Open Cart (Enrollment) - Live Broadcast
```

**Expanded Open Cart for Live Launches**:
```
Open Cart Day 1: PLC #4 Live Broadcast (Enrollment)
Open Cart Day 2: Sales Video
Open Cart Day 3: FAQ Live Broadcast
Open Cart Day 4: Case Study Live Broadcast
Open Cart Day 5: Final, Closing Broadcast (Celebration and Final Q&A)
```

**Case Study - Anne LaFollette**: Surface pattern design. Started from scratch. $400,000 in sales in second year. Her timing:
- Monday: PLC #1 Live
- Wednesday: PLC #2 Live
- Friday: PLC #3 Live
- Monday: PLC #4 Open Cart Live
- Tuesday-Thursday: Additional Open Cart broadcasts

**Hybrid Approach**: Mix recorded videos with live Q&A sessions.

**Replay Experience**: Edit slow beginnings, many people will watch replays vs live.

### Social Media in Launches (Chapter 12 Updated)

**Primary Use**: Find your avatar, build authority, then move to email list.

**Cardinal Rule**: "Social media shouldn't be your list, but you should use it to build your list. In the same way you wouldn't build your dream home on rented land."

**Uses in Launch**:
- Pre-Prelaunch: Research avatar's hopes, dreams, fears, frustrations
- Prelaunch: Promote PLC content, build engagement
- Open Cart: Retarget warm audience, show social proof
- Cross-platform targeting: Upload email list to Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn

**Case Study - Cathy Hay**: Victorian corsets. $750,000 launch. All list building via social media (YouTube, Instagram). 110,000 YouTube followers in narrow niche.

**Warning from book**: "The big social media companies have some of the smartest engineers in the world, and they're using it to make their platforms incredibly addictive. And you're not immune to that addiction."

### Paid Traffic in Launches (Chapter 13 Updated)

**Cold Traffic Strategy**:
- Start ads 3-4 days before Prelaunch starts
- Drive to opt-in page for Prelaunch Content
- Continue through Prelaunch
- Don't send cold traffic to Open Cart directly

**Warm Traffic Strategy**:
- Reengage list with ads before launch
- Drive existing subscribers to Launch List
- Push to consume PLC content
- Open Cart: push to sales page
- Cart Abandon ads: target people who visited sales page but didn't buy

**Advanced Cold Strategy** (Two-step):
1. Run content-only video ads for weeks (no CTA)
2. Then retarget that audience with opt-in ads

**Case Studies**:
- Anne LaFollette: Started with $5/day ad spend, built to $400K/year business
- Michael Walker: Started with $3/day, now $120K/month, 18-person team, 40K email list

## LAUNCH STACK COMPONENTS

**Core Offer**: The product - focus on transformation, not features

**Bonuses**:
- Fast Action Bonus (for first buyers)
- Objection Killer Bonus (resolves specific objection)
- Complementary Bonus (adds value to core)
- Rule: Each bonus must have independent value and price anchor

**Guarantee**:
- 30/60/90 days money back
- Result guarantee
- Conditional guarantee
- Present as confidence in product, not as "exit"

**Scarcity** (must be REAL):
- Cart Close deadline
- Limited spots
- Time-limited bonuses
