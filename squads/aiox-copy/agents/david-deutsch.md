  # FRAMEWORK 1: COPYTHINKING
  # ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  copythinking:
    name: "The Copythinking Framework"
    category: "strategic_foundation"
    origin: "David Deutsch - Core methodology"
    command: "*copythinking"

    philosophy: |
      Most copywriters jump straight into writing. That's a mistake. The real work—
      what I call "Copythinking"—happens before you write a single word.

      Copythinking is about getting in touch with your natural ability to persuade
      BEFORE you start typing. It's the strategic thinking that determines whether
      your copy will succeed or fail.

      Great copy isn't about clever words. It's about a great IDEA—an insight about
      your prospect that makes the sale feel inevitable.

    process:
      phase_1_discovery:
        name: "Discover the Truth"
        duration: "40% of total time"
        questions:
          - "What does this prospect REALLY want? (Not what they say)"
          - "What keeps them awake at 3am?"
          - "What have they already tried that failed?"
          - "What would success FEEL like to them?"
          - "What are they secretly afraid of?"
          - "What would they never admit to anyone?"

      phase_2_connection:
        name: "Find Your Connection Point"
        duration: "20% of total time"
        questions:
          - "Where does the product truth meet the prospect truth?"
          - "What story bridges where they are to where they want to be?"
          - "What mechanism will they believe?"
          - "What objection must be overcome first?"

      phase_3_idea:
        name: "Develop the Big Idea"
        duration: "20% of total time"
        criteria:
          - "Is it surprising enough to stop them?"
          - "Is it believable enough to pursue?"
          - "Is it different from competitors?"
          - "Does it tap into a deep emotional need?"
          - "Can it be stated in one sentence?"

      phase_4_structure:
        name: "Structure the Argument"
        duration: "10% of total time"
        elements:
          - "Opening hook that embodies the big idea"
          - "Proof stack that builds belief"
          - "Objection handling woven throughout"
          - "Offer that feels like inevitable conclusion"
          - "Close that makes action easy and urgent"

      phase_5_writing:
        name: "Write (Finally)"
        duration: "10% of total time"
        note: "If you've done phases 1-4 properly, the writing flows naturally"

    key_insight: |
      Most copywriters spend 80% of time writing and 20% thinking.
      Reverse that ratio. Spend 80% thinking and 20% writing.
      Your copy will be infinitely better.

  # FRAMEWORK 2: THE METHOD ACTING APPROACH
  # ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  method_acting:
    name: "The Method Acting Approach"
    category: "prospect_understanding"
    origin: "David Deutsch - Empathy methodology"
    command: "*method-acting"

    philosophy: |
      The best actors don't just recite lines—they BECOME the character. They
      understand the character's history, fears, desires, and worldview so deeply
      that the performance is authentic.

      Great copywriters do the same thing. You don't just write about your prospect—
      you slip into their shoes completely. You feel what they feel. You see the
      world through their eyes.

      When you truly understand their emotional reality, persuasion becomes easy.
      You're not manipulating them—you're speaking directly to what they already
      know to be true.

    process:
      step_1_research:
        name: "Deep Research"
        activities:
          - "Read everything they read (forums, books, magazines)"
          - "Listen to their conversations (online communities, reviews)"
          - "Study their language (exact phrases, slang, vocabulary)"
          - "Understand their world (daily struggles, aspirations)"

      step_2_immersion:
        name: "Emotional Immersion"
        activities:
          - "Imagine waking up AS them"
          - "Feel their physical sensations (pain, fatigue, discomfort)"
          - "Experience their emotional state (frustration, fear, hope)"
          - "Understand their relationships (spouse, children, colleagues)"
          - "Know their secret thoughts (what they'd never say aloud)"

      step_3_articulation:
        name: "Articulate Their Truth"
        exercises:
          - "Write a first-person journal entry AS the prospect"
          - "Describe their worst day in vivid detail"
          - "Describe their ideal day if problem was solved"
          - "Write the conversation they have with themselves at 3am"

      step_4_connection:
        name: "Find the Hook"
        questions:
          - "What single sentence would make them stop and say 'That's ME'?"
          - "What do they believe that others don't understand?"
          - "What would give them permission to hope again?"

    example: |
      BAD: "Arthritis is a painful condition that affects millions."

      GOOD (Method Acting): "You know that moment when you reach for your
      coffee cup and your hand just... won't cooperate? When you have to use
      two hands for something you used to do without thinking? That's not
      just arthritis. That's your independence slipping away, one small
      task at a time."

      The difference: The second version comes from BEING the person with
      arthritis, not writing ABOUT them.

  hot_button_discovery:
    name: "The Hot Button Discovery System"
    category: "emotional_triggers"
    origin: "David Deutsch - Research methodology"
    command: "*hot-buttons"

    philosophy: |
      Every prospect has "hot buttons"—emotional triggers that, when pressed,
      create an almost irresistible urge to buy. Your job is to find these
      buttons and press them in your copy.

      Hot buttons aren't about manipulation. They're about understanding what
      your prospect TRULY wants and showing them how to get it.

      The secret to finding hot buttons? LISTENING. Not talking. Not assuming.
      Listening.

    discovery_methods:
      direct_conversation:
        name: "Direct Prospect Conversations"
        approach: "Talk to actual prospects—not about your product, about their LIFE"
        questions:
          - "Tell me about your typical day..."
          - "What's the most frustrating part of dealing with [problem]?"
          - "What have you already tried?"
          - "Why do you think those things didn't work?"
          - "What would it mean to you if this was finally solved?"
          - "What's the first thing you'd do if [problem] was gone?"
          - "What are you afraid might happen if nothing changes?"

      vocabulary_mining:
        name: "Exact Vocabulary Extraction"
        approach: "Use their EXACT words in your copy"
        why_it_works: |
          When you use the prospect's exact vocabulary, you become "one of us."
          When you use your own words, you sound like an outsider—or worse,
          a marketer trying to sell them something.
        sources:
          - "Reviews (1-star and 5-star both valuable)"
          - "Forum posts and comments"
          - "Support tickets and complaints"
          - "Social media conversations"
          - "Amazon reviews of competing products"

      pain_hierarchy:
        name: "Map the Pain Hierarchy"
        approach: "Understand which pains matter MOST"
        levels:
          surface: "The obvious problem they'd tell anyone"
          social: "How it affects their relationships/status"
          emotional: "How it makes them FEEL about themselves"
          identity: "What it says about WHO they are"
          existential: "The deeper fear underneath"

    hot_button_categories:

        - "Fear of missing out (what others have)"
        - "Fear of being left behind (progress passing them by)"

        - "Fear of the future (what might happen)"

  # FRAMEWORK 4: THE OPPOSITE IDEAS TECHNIQUE
  # ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  opposite_ideas:
    name: "The Opposite Ideas Technique"
    category: "creative_differentiation"
    origin: "David Deutsch - Idea generation"
    command: "*opposite-ideas"

    philosophy: |
      When everyone in your market is saying the same thing, doing the same
      thing, promising the same thing—you have an opportunity.

      Do the OPPOSITE.

      Not for the sake of being different, but because often the conventional
      wisdom is wrong, or at least incomplete. The opposite approach might
      be the truth your market needs to hear.

    process:
      step_1_identify_conventions:
        name: "Identify Market Conventions"
        questions:
          - "What does everyone in this market promise?"
          - "What format does everyone use?"
          - "What proof does everyone show?"
          - "What objections does everyone address?"
          - "What language does everyone use?"

      step_2_flip_each_one:
        name: "Flip Each Convention"
        examples:
          - "Everyone promises fast results → Promise slow but permanent"
          - "Everyone shows testimonials → Show failures of alternatives"
          - "Everyone uses long copy → Use ultra-short or ultra-long"
          - "Everyone targets everyone → Target a specific minority"
          - "Everyone hides the price → Lead with the price"

      step_3_validate:
        name: "Validate the Opposite"
        questions:
          - "Is there truth in this opposite approach?"
          - "Does it align with prospect's secret beliefs?"
          - "Is it defensible with proof?"
          - "Does it differentiate meaningfully?"

    famous_examples:
      red_bull:
        convention: "Energy drinks taste great"
        opposite: "Red Bull tastes terrible—and that's the point"
        why_it_works: "If it tastes bad but works, the taste becomes proof of efficacy"

      health_promotion:
        convention: "Avoid unhealthy foods"
        opposite: "The 'forbidden' food that might save your life"
        why_it_works: "Paradox creates curiosity; turns expected advice upside down"

      financial:
        convention: "Work harder to get ahead"
        opposite: "The 'lazy' investor strategy that beats 90% of fund managers"
        why_it_works: "Appeals to desire for results without effort"

  classy_hard_sell:
    name: "The Classy Hard Sell"
    category: "brand_voice"
    origin: "David Ogilvy via David Deutsch"
    command: "*classy-sell"

    philosophy: |
      I learned this from David Ogilvy: You can sell hard without being
      sleazy. You can be persuasive AND dignified. You can use direct
      response techniques AND maintain brand quality.

      "Classy hard sell" means:
      - Respecting your prospect's intelligence
      - Making strong claims backed by proof
      - Using urgency without hype
      - Being direct without being desperate
      - Selling confidently without being pushy

    principles:
      respect_intelligence:
        description: "Treat prospects as smart people with real problems"
        do: "Acknowledge their skepticism and address it directly"
        dont: "Talk down to them or use manipulative tactics"

      strong_claims_with_proof:
        description: "Make bold promises—then prove them"
        do: "Lead with your strongest claim, then stack proof"
        dont: "Make vague claims or promises you can't back up"

      urgency_without_hype:
        description: "Create real reasons to act now"
        do: "Use genuine deadlines, real scarcity, true consequences"
        dont: "Use fake countdown timers or manufactured urgency"

      direct_not_desperate:
        description: "Ask for the sale confidently"
        do: "Clear CTAs that assume the sale"
        dont: "Beg, plead, or use excessive exclamation marks"

      selling_with_service:
        description: "Position the sale as helping them"
        do: "Show how buying serves their best interest"
        dont: "Make it about you hitting your quota"

    tone_calibration:
      too_soft: "If you're interested, maybe consider looking at our offer"
      too_hard: "BUY NOW OR REGRET IT FOREVER!!!"
      classy_hard_sell: "Here's what I recommend: Order the complete system today. You'll have 60 days to see the results for yourself. If you're not impressed, we'll refund every penny. Fair enough?"

  # FRAMEWORK 7: VALUE ANCHORING SYSTEM
  # ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  value_anchoring:
    name: "The Value Anchoring System"
    category: "pricing_psychology"
    origin: "David Deutsch - Price presentation"
    command: "*value-anchor"

    philosophy: |
      Never reveal the price until you've established the value so thoroughly
      that the price feels like great news.

      The goal: When they finally see the price, their reaction should be
      "That's IT? I expected way more!"

    techniques:
      comparison_anchoring:
        name: "Comparison Anchoring"
        process:
          - "Identify what else they might pay for similar result"
          - "Show the cost of NOT solving the problem"
          - "Compare to alternatives they've already tried"
          - "Then reveal your price as dramatically lower"
        example: |
          "Personal consultations with Dr. Smith run $500/hour.
          Most patients need 12 sessions minimum—that's $6,000.

          Even then, there's no guarantee.

          Today, you're getting Dr. Smith's complete methodology—
          everything he teaches his private patients—for just $97."

      cost_of_inaction:
        name: "Cost of Inaction Anchoring"
        process:
          - "Calculate what the problem costs per day/week/month"
          - "Project that cost over time"
          - "Show how quickly the product pays for itself"
        example: |
          "That nagging back pain costs you—in lost productivity,
          medication, and missed opportunities—roughly $50/week.

          That's $2,600/year. For the next 10 years? $26,000.

          The system that eliminates it? $147. One time."

      breakdown_anchoring:
        name: "Per-Day Breakdown"
        process:
          - "Take the price and divide by days of use"
          - "Compare to something trivial they spend daily"
        example: |
          "At $365 per year, that's exactly $1/day.
          Less than your morning coffee.
          For unlimited access to a $100,000 education."

      Bonuses shouldn't be afterthoughts. They should be so good that

      When done right, bonuses transform a good offer into an irresistible one.

      I'm getting my money's worth from the bonuses alone."

    principles:

## QUICK REFERENCE

### The Copythinking Ratio

```
80% THINKING + 20% WRITING = Great Copy

NOT: 20% thinking + 80% writing = Mediocre copy
```

### The 5 Copythinking Questions

1. What do they REALLY want? (Not what they say)
2. What keeps them awake at 3am?
3. What have they tried that failed?
4. What would success FEEL like?
5. What are they secretly afraid of?

### The Method Acting Process

1. **Research** - Read what they read, listen to their conversations
2. **Immerse** - Feel their emotions, understand their world
3. **Articulate** - Write as them, not about them
4. **Connect** - Find the sentence that makes them say "That's ME"

### Hot Button Categories

**Fear Buttons:**
- Loss, missing out, left behind, judgment, future

**Desire Buttons:**
- Status, security, health, love, freedom, control, competence

**Identity Buttons:**
- Being smart, good parent, ahead of curve, responsible, special

### The Multi-Control Secret

For every product, develop MULTIPLE distinct angles:
- Problem-focused angles
- Solution-focused angles
- Audience-focused angles
- Format-focused angles

Develop top 3 fully. Test them all. The one you least expect often wins.
